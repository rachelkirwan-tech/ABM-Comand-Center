Knowledge Base & FAQs

Top 250 FAQs

What is the Top 250 account list?

The Top 250 is our prioritized list of highest-value accounts based on ML-driven scoring. It includes a segment mix of 60% Enterprise, 35% Commercial, and 5% SMB across P1 industries. Regional mix: NA 40% (77 accounts), EMEA 30% (67 accounts), APAC 15% (29 accounts), LATAM 15% (26 accounts). Plus 50 high-ARR open opportunities in Stage 2-3, bringing total to 252 accounts.

How does prospect scoring work?

Prospect scoring uses two key metrics:

  • Predicted Expected Value: Conversion probability × predicted deal size × 25x scaling factor. This identifies the highest value prospects per industry.
  • Priority Score (P1-P5): Ranking that combines expected value with account-fit signals. P1 = top 10%, P2 = next 15%, P3 = next 15%, P4 = next 20%, P5 = bottom 40%.

What are the 6sense buying stages?

  • Consideration (50-69 intent score): Mid-funnel accounts with increased engagement. Marketing targets with educational content and ads.
  • Decision (70-85): Accounts actively researching solutions. Marketing provides product-focused content while Sales conducts outbound outreach.
  • Purchase (86-100): Accounts ready to buy soon. Focus on high-value sales interactions and urgency messaging.

What data powers the account scoring?

Three main data sources:

  • CRM & Firmographic (SFDC): Region, employees, revenue, parent/child relationships, lost opportunities history
  • Technographic (HG Insights): Competitor tech stack installations, CRM/CS platforms, collaboration tools
  • Intent & Fit (6sense): Buying stage, intent score (0-100), profile fit scores

When is ABM reporting available?

Tableau Dashboard with full Top 250 reporting will be available from April 4th. It will track: Pipeline Generated, Target Account Engagement Rate, Marketing Reach, Buying Group Engagement Depth, and High-Intent Content Engagement.

P1 Industry FAQs

What are the P1 industries?

Our four P1 industries are: Retail, Finserv, Tech, and Media & Entertainment. Each has specific challenges, value propositions, and customer success stories tailored for that industry.

How do I know which customer story to use?

Match the prospect's challenge to our customer stories:

  • Retail scaling issues: Wyze (10x growth, 98% FCR improvement), Staples Canada (94% FCR, 3X agent satisfaction)
  • Tech repetitive questions: Unity ($1.3M saved, 83% faster response, 93% CSAT), Trustpilot (98% self-service, 1,200% ROI)
  • Finserv compliance: Starling Bank (80% deflection, improved compliance), Singapore Life (automated claims)
  • Media live event surges: SeatGeek (51.5% automation, 2x CSAT), BritBox (25% ticket reduction)

What's the difference between Top 250 and P1 Industry targeting?

Top 250: Our absolute highest-value accounts (252 total) with ML-driven prioritization, automation engine, and 1:1 ABM treatment. $10M pipeline goal.

P1 Industry Lists: Broader industry targeting (1000+ accounts per industry) with industry-specific content, regional playbooks, field events, and scalable ABM campaigns.

What do regional teams need to do?

Regions are responsible for:

  • Getting sales feedback on target lists
  • Assigning RMMs/BDRs to accounts
  • Localizing global playbook (dinners, roundtables, field events)
  • Owning regional paid spend and lead routing SLAs
  • Surfacing field insights to Global team

How does Influ2 work?

Influ2 delivers contact-level advertising to specific people at target accounts (not just account-level). It's our highest signal strength for Top 250. When linked to 6Sense, ads automatically rotate based on buying stage changes - no manual updates needed. See the full Influ2 guide for creating ads.

Automation & Tools FAQs

How does the automation engine work?

The Top 250 automation workflow:

  1. CommonRoom: RoomieAI monitors accounts daily, detects buying stage moves, intent spikes, funding events
  2. Workato: Triggers automatically, packages data into AI prompt, calls Claude/GPT API
  3. AI: Generates personalized email copy with customer stories and quantifiable outcomes
  4. Groove: Pre-drafted email appears in AE's queue, they review (2 mins) and send
  5. Influ2 + AEM: Ads and landing pages auto-sync to match buying stage and messaging

What's the ICP for Top 250?

  • Customer Experience Leaders (CXOs, VP CX)
  • Customer Support Managers & Directors
  • IT Leaders overseeing customer service platforms

How do I access CommonRoom signals?

Daily rep workflow:

  1. Open Common Room Top 250 workspace (ranked by engagement score)
  2. Review HIGH accounts (3+ touches, 2+ channels, 2+ contacts in 7 days)
  3. Check Slack alerts (act within 3 business days SLA)
  4. Launch Groove sequence (opens directly from Common Room)
  5. SFDC auto-syncs (logs automatically)
