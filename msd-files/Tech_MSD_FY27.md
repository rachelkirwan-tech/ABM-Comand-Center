## Terminology

- "Technical Support" vs. "Customer Service": Technical support = ongoing setup/troubleshooting (break/fix). Customer service = broader help. Tech companies use both.
- "SLA" vs. "SLO": SLA = contract commitment to customers. SLO = internal analysis for common CS.
- "MTTR" vs. "Average Resolution Time": Same concept, MTTR is engineering/ops phrasing.
- "Incident/Outage" vs. "Issue": Incident = service-impacting event needing coordinated response. Issue = general request/problem.
- "Support Engineer" and "Engineering Escalation": Support engineer troubleshoots deeply. Escalation goes to engineering for product/system fixes.

## Key Challenges

**Fragmented tech stacks and data silos:**
- Discovery: "How many systems do you use to manage support operations?"
- Value: Unify your support ecosystem to unlock technical agility. Integrate with prebuilt and customizable connectors, multiple channels, and unified knowledge base.
- Customer proof: GitHub (AMER, ENT) improved visibility, planning, and collaboration. Catapult (APAC, COM) reduced ticket handling time by 2-7 minutes.

**Scaling all support:**
- Discovery: "If you doubled your customer count, would you maintain service quality without doubling headcount?"
- Value: Drive consistent, premier service and protect agent bandwidth with AI-powered workflows.
- Customer proof: Degreed (AMER, COM) achieved 75% reduction in SLA violations. TeamSystem (EMEA, ENT) automated up to 80% of repetitive inquiries.

**Inconsistent KPIs:**
- Discovery: "When leadership asks for a report from support, how long does it take and how much do you trust it?"
- Value: Optimize AI-powered, actionable insights to accelerate business outcomes.
- Customer proof: Docebo (AMER, COM) evaluates 100% of interactions automatically, 50% less time per evaluation. Degreed saved $1M through automation and reduced volume by 5,000 monthly tickets.

**Lacking confidence in AI governance, compliance and security:**
- Discovery: "What security or compliance considerations are top of mind as you scale support?"
- Value: 82% of B2B buyers expect safe, secure handling of information (Deloitte/Forrester). Solidify trust with protected infrastructure.
- Customer proof: Calendly (AMER, COM) centralized and secured internal service operations. Indigov (AMER, SMB) inherited compliance controls out of the box for FedRAMP.
