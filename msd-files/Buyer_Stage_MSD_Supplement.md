## Stage Definitions

### Awareness
**What it means:** The account is in scope. They fit the ICP. They may be on the Top 250 list or in a P1 industry segment. But there is no meaningful engagement signal yet. 6sense classifies them as target or awareness stage. They are under a rock.

**What happened to put them here:** Nothing yet. They exist in our universe because of firmographic fit, industry match, or list inclusion. They have not visited the website, engaged with content, or shown keyword intent beyond baseline noise.

**Our goal:** Introduce the industry problem. Plant the Zendesk POV. Create the first signal. We do not expect a form fill. We do not expect a click. We expect an impression that starts building familiarity.

**Derived from Lindsay (Feb 3):** "In scope is like all of ABX, all of industry top 500, that is the air cover. That is what they are just getting. All industry, CX, that kind of topic. Awareness is where they get that air cover."

**Derived from Rachel K (Mar 25):** "We do not have MSDs by stage. An MSD around the funnel of what do we know about our target account that is in awareness. What should we be talking to them about based off their awareness of Zendesk. Where do we start the conversation."

---

### Consideration
**What it means:** The account has shown engagement. They visited the website. They attended a webinar. They downloaded an asset. A persona from the account showed keyword intent on 6sense. Common Room flagged a signal. They moved from passive to active. 6sense classifies them as consideration or early decision.

**What happened to put them here:** One or more of these happened: industry webinar registration or attendance, key asset download (workbook, report), website visit pattern (multiple visits or deep pages), keyword research matching Zendesk branded or pain terms, engagement with Influ2 awareness ads (prioritized for sales threshold reached), event attendance (industry breakfast, Relate session, field event).

**Our goal:** Prove that teams like them are solving this with Zendesk. Show use cases, results, patterns. Surface momentum to sales via Common Room. Move them toward a conversation. The content gets more specific. CTAs get warmer.

**Derived from Lindsay (Feb 3):** "The value proposition for consideration is how are teams like them, or how are teams like me, addressing this challenge. Use cases, patterns. Customer stories can live here. We surface that momentum to sales at this point."

**Derived from Pavlo/Rachel (Mar 9):** "Think through what they see first, what they see second. If they click on an ad, they will see another ad. At a step of sales engagement, start showing them more middle or bottom of funnel content."

**Derived from 6sense workshop (Jan 26):** "As they start moving down funnel, pushing them to gated content is a more expensive channel. The messaging is getting specific about your products versus your competitors."

---

### Decision
**What it means:** The account is hot. Multiple personas are engaged. There is an MQL or active opportunity signal. Sales is already aware. BDRs have been notified through Common Room high-intent signals. 6sense classifies them as decision or purchase. The buying group is forming or formed.

**What happened to put them here:** Multiple signals converged: MQL from a persona at the account, active opportunity in Salesforce, multiple buying committee members engaged (CX leader plus IT plus C-suite), branded keyword research (Zendesk product terms, competitive comparisons), demo page visit or trial sign-up, sales outreach already in progress via Groove sequence.

**Our goal:** Reinforce the decision. Remove friction. Speak directly to outcomes and speed to value. CTAs are direct. Ads should feel like they are already in a conversation with Zendesk, not discovering it. Sales plays are active alongside these ads.

**Derived from Lindsay (Feb 3):** "We do not want sales to wait to decision. We want sales coming in at consideration. They are noticing this retargeting engagement and using that signal to send personalized outreach."

**Derived from 6sense workshop (Jan 26), Veeam example:** "If the account was in consideration, it was going to be a follow up to the thought leadership piece, a little bit more specific. On down, they built out three different tracks. They relied on buying stage to drive how deep they got with the second and third campaign."

**Derived from Rachel K (Mar 25):** "Let us say they are in the decision stage. We can automatically generate the ads based on their industry and their buying stage."

## Ad Copy Direction by Stage

### Global Copy Rules (All Stages)

Never use the phrase "Zendesk AI" in any ad copy. The correct terminology is "AI agents" or simply "AI" when referencing Zendesk's AI capabilities. "Zendesk AI" is an internal product name, not customer-facing ad language. Also never use "AI-powered" as a modifier. Say what the AI does, not that it is AI-powered.

---

### Awareness Stage Copy Rules

**Headline approach:** Lead with the industry tension or a stat that reframes the problem. The buyer does not know they need Zendesk. The headline should make them pause and think about their own situation.

**Body approach:** Industry POV. Frame the challenge. Reference a data point from the industry MSD. Do not mention Zendesk product names. Do not mention features. Speak to the category problem.

**CTA strength:** Soft. Educational. No commitment implied.
- "Read now"
- "See the data"
- "Explore the report"
- "Get the insights"

**Content destination:** Industry workbook, industry insights report, ungated blog content, industry landing page hub.

**Tone:** Authoritative but not salesy. Think analyst report headline, not vendor pitch.

**What to avoid:** Product names, feature language, demo references, pricing, competitive claims, anything that feels like a sales motion.

**Example headlines (Retail, for reference):**
- Stat-Led: "Retailers lose over 30% of customers to slow support"
- Tension-Led: "Your checkout works. Your support does not."

**Example headlines (M&E, for reference):**
- Stat-Led: "AI resolves over 50% of fan inquiries automatically"
- Tension-Led: "Fans will not wait. Neither should your team."

---

### Consideration Stage Copy Rules

**Headline approach:** Lead with proof or a result. The buyer knows the problem exists. They are evaluating how others solve it. Headlines should reference outcomes, peer behavior, or specific capabilities without being a feature dump.

**Body approach:** Social proof. Customer patterns. Industry-specific use cases from the MSD. Reference what teams like theirs are doing. Include a Zendesk mention but frame it as the enabler, not the hero. The customer is the hero.

**CTA strength:** Mid-funnel. Invites deeper engagement without demanding a sales conversation.
- "See how it works"
- "Get the guide"
- "Watch the demo"
- "Read the case study"
- "Join the conversation"

**Content destination:** Solution pages, customer story pages, demo video, industry workbook (gated), webinar replay, Relate session content.

**Tone:** Confident and specific. Evidence-based. Think "here is what your peers are doing" not "here is what we sell."

**What to avoid:** Generic value props, broad claims without specificity, awareness-level framing (they are past that), hard sales CTAs (they are not there yet).

**Example headlines (Retail, for reference):**
- Stat-Led: "Top retailers cut resolution time by over 40% with AI"
- Tension-Led: "Your competitors automated holiday surges. Did you?"

**Example headlines (M&E, for reference):**
- Stat-Led: "Streaming teams resolve over 80% of billing issues without agents"
- Tension-Led: "Your rivals scaled for launch day. Can your support?"

---

### Decision Stage Copy Rules

**Headline approach:** Lead with the outcome and urgency. The buyer is close. They are comparing options. Headlines should feel like they are answering the final objection or reinforcing the decision already being made. Be specific about ROI, speed, or competitive differentiation.

**Body approach:** Direct value. Speed to value. ROI or efficiency metrics from the MSD. Competitive differentiation if relevant. Frame Zendesk as the obvious choice. The body should feel like it is confirming what they already suspect.

**CTA strength:** Direct. Action-oriented. Implies a conversation or commitment.
- "Talk to an expert"
- "Get a demo"
- "See your ROI"
- "Start now"
- "Book a consultation"

**Content destination:** Demo request page, ROI calculator, custom demo, sales-led landing page, pricing page.

**Tone:** Confident and urgent. Direct without being pushy. Think trusted advisor closing the loop, not cold outreach.

**What to avoid:** Educational framing (they do not need to learn anymore), soft CTAs (they need a next step), broad industry POV (they need Zendesk-specific proof), anything that resets them to awareness.

**Example headlines (Retail, for reference):**
- Stat-Led: "Teams using Zendesk AI see ROI in under 30 days"
- Tension-Led: "Every day without AI support costs you customers"

**Example headlines (M&E, for reference):**
- Stat-Led: "Go live with AI agents in weeks, not months"
- Tension-Led: "Your next launch is coming. Is your support ready?"

## Integration Notes for Intelligence Layer

### Prompt Logic
When the buyer stage dropdown is selected, append the following instruction to the Bedrock prompt:

```
The selected buyer stage is [STAGE]. Use the stage-specific copy rules from the Buyer Stage MSD Supplement:
- Awareness: industry tension or stat headlines, industry POV body, soft CTAs (Read now, See the data), no product names
- Consideration: proof and result headlines, social proof body with Zendesk as enabler, mid-funnel CTAs (See how it works, Get the guide)  
- Decision: outcome and urgency headlines, direct value body with ROI/speed, direct CTAs (Get a demo, Talk to an expert)

Adjust headline approach, body tone, CTA text, and content framing based on the selected stage. The industry MSD provides the WHAT (stats, pain points, benefits). The buyer stage determines the HOW (tone, urgency, specificity, CTA strength).
```

### MSD Interaction
The industry MSD and the buyer stage supplement are complementary inputs. The MSD provides industry-specific stats, pain points, benefit pillars, and proof points. The stage supplement tells the AI how aggressively to use them:
- Awareness: use stats to introduce the problem
- Consideration: use stats to prove others are solving it
- Decision: use stats to quantify the cost of inaction

### Default Behavior
If no buyer stage is selected, default to Awareness. This preserves backward compatibility with existing ad generation that assumes top-of-funnel positioning.
